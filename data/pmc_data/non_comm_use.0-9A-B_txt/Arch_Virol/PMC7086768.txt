Single radial haemolysis for the determination of antibody to reoviruses
D'Ambrosio E.  Battaglia M. F.  Midulla M.  C.N.R. Centre for the Study of Respiratory Viruses, Rome, Italy 
1981 
68 3 309 312
5 11 1979 3 3 1981 © Springer-Verlag 1981This article is made available via the PMC Open Access Subset for unrestricted research re-use and secondary analysis in any form or by any means with acknowledgement of the original source. These permissions are granted for the duration of the World Health Organization (WHO) declaration of COVID-19 as a global pandemic.Summary
A single radial haemolysis (SRH) for reovirus antibody determination was developed and compared to standard haemagglutination-inhibition (HI) and complement fixation (CF) techniques. SRH appeared more simple and sensitive than CF, but less sensitive and less specific than HI.
