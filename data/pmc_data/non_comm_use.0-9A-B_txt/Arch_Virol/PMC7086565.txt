Fatty acid acylation of viral proteins in murine hepatitis virus-infected cells
van Berlo M. F.  van den Brink W. J.  Horzinek M. C.  van der Zeijst B. A. M.  grid.5477.10000000120346234Institute of Virology, Veterinary Faculty, State University of Utrecht, Utrecht, The Netherlands

These permissions are granted for the duration of the World Health Organization (WHO) declaration of COVID-19 as a global pandemic.Summary
The fatty acid acylation of the cell-associated virus-specific proteins of mouse hepatitis virus (A 59-strain) was studied.3H-palmitate label was associated with E 2, one of the two virion glycoproteins and its intracellular precursor gp 150. A 110 K protein, the unglycosylated apoprotein of gp 150, accumulated by tunicamycin treatment, also incorporated radiolabeled palmitic acid. The addition of fatty acid to the MHV-A 59 E 2 protein is therefore not dependent on glycosylation.
