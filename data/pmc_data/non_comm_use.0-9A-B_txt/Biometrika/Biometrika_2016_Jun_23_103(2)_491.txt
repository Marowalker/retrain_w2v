We would also like to clarify that equivalent definitions of the missing data being missing always at random and being missing always completely at random appeared in Seaman et al. (2013) as the data being everywhere missing at random and being everywhere missing completely at random, respectively. We have chosen to use the adverb always in order to avoid confusion with the measure-theoretic concept of almost everywhere, with which the missing always at random concept has nothing in common.
