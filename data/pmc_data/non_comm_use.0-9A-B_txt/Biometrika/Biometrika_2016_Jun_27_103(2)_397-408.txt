In this article, we derive the exact joint distribution of an exchangeable multinomial vector, making no assumption beyond exchangeability in the distribution of outcomes. We obtain the exact form of the covariance matrix and show that its structure is different from what is usually assumed. In non-Bayesian applications that use quasilikelihood and generalized estimating equations for estimating marginal means, some authors adopt a form for the exchangeable multinomial covariance matrix which inflates the covariance matrix of the standard multinomial distribution by a constant factor (Ryan, 1992; Zhu et al., 1994). Indeed, in the latest version of SAS (SAS Institute, Inc., 2013) only this form of the independent working correlation matrix is available in the Genmod procedure. Other authors assume models for the covariance matrix that capitalize on study-specific features such as time-ordering of the measurements (Touloumis et al., 2013).
